AI Chatbot

Building an Intelligent Conversational Agent

The rise of large language models (LLMs) has disrupted the public awareness of the capabilities of an AI, especially after the launch of ChatGPT. On a somewhat less disruptive note but nonetheless fascinating I was working on the task of creating an AI chatbot that can have conversations with humans about movies. Being a relative novice to working with (pre-trained) LLMs, this was certainly no easy task, but was definitely worth it. My journey began by researching existing natural language processing techniques and machine learning algorithms that could be used to train my bot. One key aspect of building this bot was using a large knowledge graph as its foundation. The knowledge graph contained vast amounts of structured and unstructured data about movies, including casts, genres, awards won, budgets, box office performance, critical acclaim, and audience reception. By leveraging a large knowledge graph, my bot had access to millions of facts and insights related to movies, allowing it to respond intelligently to queries like "What is Angelina Jolie’s latest film?" or "Which action comedy won three Oscars at the Academy Awards?". To further enhance the bot's capabilities, I employed advanced Named Entity Recognition models which enabled the bot to identify and extract entities from freeform text inputs. For example, in response to the query “Who directed Spiderman 2019?”, the bot correctly extracted Tom Wattles name from raw text input and returned his correct identity. With these cutting edge technologies at hand, my chatbot was well equipped to handle any questions coming its way. Overall, creating this AI chatbot has been a challenging yet rewarding experience, and I am excited to see where it goes next! Not convinced yet by the usefulness of LLMs? The fact that this blog post was written by Llama, an open-source LLM, might further prove just how powerful these models can be and, additionally, the following conversation I held with my movie chatbot will shed some light on the status quo of the capabilities intelligent agents feature nowadays.
